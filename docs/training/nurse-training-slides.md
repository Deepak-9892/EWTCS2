# Nurse Training Slides: EWTCS

## Module 1: Introduction to EWTCS
### Purpose and Vision
- **Visibility**: Real-time tracking of all emergency ward beds.
- **Discipline**: Automated time tracking for patient stages.
- **Reporting**: Data-driven insights to improve hospital operations.

> [!NOTE]
> EWTCS is designed to *assist* coordination, not replace medical judgment.

---

## Module 2: The Nurse Dashboard
### Understanding the Bed Grid
- **Bed Tiles**: Show Bed Number, Current Stage, and Elapsed Time.
- **Color Coding**:
  - `Yellow`: Patient Admitted
  - `Orange`: Under Assessment
  - `Blue`: Tests Ordered
  - `Purple`: Awaiting Results
  - `Green`: Decision Made
  - `Red`: **DEFERRED/DELAYED** (Exceeds time limit)

---

## Module 3: Daily Workflow
### Admission to Discharge
1. **Admit**: Click the initial Bed Status to start the timer.
2. **Move Stage**: Click the current stage to progress (e.g., Assessment -> Tests).
3. **Discharge**: Final stage stops the timer and clears the bed tile.

---

## Module 4: Recording Delays
### Acknowledging Red Flags
- When a tile turns **Red**, it indicates a bottleneck.
- You may be prompted to select a **Disposition Delay Reason** (e.g., Awaiting Lab, Bed Availability).
- Selecting a reason helps management identify and fix systemic issues.

---

## Module 5: Virtual Beds
### Managing Overflow
- Use the **"Add Virtual Bed"** button for patients in hallways or on stretchers.
- These function exactly like fixed beds but are designated as "Virtual".
- **Discharge** the virtual bed once the patient is moved or sent home.

---

## Summary & Support
- **Goal**: Keep the dashboard updated in *one click*.
- **Sync**: Updates reflect instantly across all station screens.
- **Support**: Contact your Ward Supervisor for any technical issues.
